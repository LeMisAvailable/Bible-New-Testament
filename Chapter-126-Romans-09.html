The Potter's Hand

Romans Chapter 9

Duration: ~2 minutes | Style: Devotional Ballad

[Verse 1]

My heart carries great sorrow deep,
For my brothers, Israel's sheep,
Theirs the covenant, the law, the light,
The promises shining ever bright.
Yet not all who descend are true,
God's purpose stands in all He'll do.

[Chorus]

Who are you, O man, to question God?
The clay cannot speak back to the rod,
The Potter shapes with sovereign hand,
His mercy flows across the land.

[Verse 2]

Jacob I loved, before his birth,
Before he proved or showed his worth,
Not by works but by the call,
God's election covers all.
I will have mercy on whom I choose,
Compassion flows, none can refuse.

[Chorus]

Who are you, O man, to question God?
The clay cannot speak back to the rod,
The Potter shapes with sovereign hand,
His mercy flows across the land.

[Bridge]

Israel pursued the law of right,
But stumbled in the fading light,
The stone of stumbling in their way,
Yet those who trust will never sway.

[Outro]

When fearful thoughts are intertwined,
You calm the storm inside my mind.
Jesus, though I cannot see Your hand,
Your faithful heart I understand.
