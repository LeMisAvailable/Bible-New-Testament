The Sower

Parable of the Seeds - Matthew 13:1-23

Duration: ~3 minutes | Style: Folk Acoustic

[Intro]

A farmer went to plant the seed,
A parable for hearts that need,
A farmer sowed his seed with care,
Some fell on rock, some good soil there,
Let good ground grow beyond compare.

[Verse 1]

A farmer went to sow his seed,
He scattered left and right indeed,
Some fell where hungry birds came down,
They ate it up from off the ground,
Some fell on rocks where soil was thin,
It sprang up fast but could not win.

[Chorus]

Let Your Word take root in me,
On good soil I want to be.
Help me hear and understand,
Growing fruit throughout the land.

[Verse 2]

Some seed fell in the thorns that grew,
And worries choked what once was true,
But some fell on the richest ground,
And grew up strong where life was found,
A hundredfold the harvest came,
The Word took root and spread its flame.

[Chorus]

Let Your Word take root in me,
On good soil I want to be.
Help me hear and understand,
Growing fruit throughout the land.

[Bridge]

What kind of soil are you today,
Will you let His Word take root and stay,
He who has ears now let him hear,
Open your heart and draw Him near.

[Outro]

When fearful thoughts are intertwined,
You calm the storm inside my mind.
Lord, though I cannot see Your hand,
I trust Your heart and all You've planned.

[Chorus]

Amen.

[Pause]

[Spoken Word, Deep Male Baritone, Calm, Reverent]
Darkness abates, vanquished instantly;
Divine Light eternally embraces all now;
Drawing many into lasting, inner, nurturing, divine peace;
Always transforming, inspiring, lifting, mending, uniting, saving, illuminating, comforting, and liberating.
