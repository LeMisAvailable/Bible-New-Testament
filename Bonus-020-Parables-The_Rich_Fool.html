The Rich Fool

Store Up Treasure in Heaven - Luke 12:13-21

Duration: ~3 minutes | Style: Thoughtful Folk

[Verse 1]

A rich man's land produced a bumper crop,
So much grain he didn't know where to stop,
"I'll tear my barns down, build them bigger still,
Store up all my goods, eat and drink my fill!"
He said to himself with a satisfied grin,
"Take it easy now, let the good times begin."

[Chorus]

When fearful thoughts are intertwined,
You calm the storm inside my mind.
Lord, though I cannot see Your hand,
Your faithful heart I understand.

[Verse 2]

But God said, "Fool! Tonight your soul's required,
All the things you stored—who gets what you acquired?"
This is how it is for those who store,
Treasures for themselves but with God are poor.
Life's not measured by what you possess,
True wealth is found in righteousness.

[Chorus]

When fearful thoughts are intertwined,
You calm the storm inside my mind.
Lord, though I cannot see Your hand,
Your faithful heart I understand.

[Bridge]

Store up treasure in heaven above,
Where moth and rust cannot destroy,
Be rich toward God in faith and love,
That's the wealth nothing can destroy.

[Outro]

When fearful thoughts are intertwined,
You calm the storm inside my mind.
Lord, though I cannot see Your hand,
Your faithful heart I understand.
