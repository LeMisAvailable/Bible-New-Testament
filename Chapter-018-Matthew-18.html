The Heart of Forgiveness

Matthew Chapter 18

Duration: ~2 minutes | Style: Gentle Worship

[Verse 1]

"Who is the greatest?" the disciples inquired,
Jesus called a child, His words inspired,
"Unless you change and become like these,
You cannot enter—humble on your knees.
Whoever welcomes a child in My name,
Welcomes Me—we are one and the same."

[Chorus]

Seventy times seven, forgive again,
As you've been forgiven, release the pain,
For where two or three gather in My name,
There I am with them, forever the same.

[Verse 2]

If your brother sins, go and speak alone,
If he listens, you've gained him—love has grown,
Whatever you bind on earth is bound above,
Whatever you loose is free through love.
A shepherd leaves ninety-nine for one,
Rejoicing when the lost sheep's found and won.

[Chorus]

Seventy times seven, forgive again,
As you've been forgiven, release the pain,
For where two or three gather in My name,
There I am with them, forever the same.

[Bridge]

A servant owed millions, debt forgiven full,
Yet choked a fellow servant—his heart so cruel,
The master took back mercy he'd first shown,
Forgive from your heart, or stand alone.

[Outro]

When fearful thoughts are intertwined,
You calm the storm inside my mind.
Lord, though I cannot see Your hand,
Your faithful heart I understand.
