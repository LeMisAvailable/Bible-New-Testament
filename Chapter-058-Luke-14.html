The Great Banquet

Luke Chapter 14

Duration: ~2 minutes | Style: Devotional Ballad

[Verse 1]

Take the lowest seat, He said,
Let humility be your bread,
For those who lift themselves up high,
Will fall beneath the Master's eye.
Invite the poor, the lame, the blind,
And treasures in heaven you will find.

[Chorus]

Come to the banquet, the table is spread,
The hungry and broken will surely be fed,
The highways and hedges are calling your name,
No excuse is worth missing this glorious flame.
Count the cost, take up your cross,
Without Him, everything is loss.

[Verse 2]

A man prepared a feast so grand,
But guests refused his outstretched hand,
One bought a field, one bought some oxen,
One was wed—their hearts were locked in.
So from the streets the poor were brought,
To fill the seats the rich had sought.

[Chorus]

Come to the banquet, the table is spread,
The hungry and broken will surely be fed,

[Bridge]

If salt has lost its taste and fire,
What use is it but to expire?
Whoever has ears, let them hear,
The call of Christ is drawing near.
Hate your life to save your soul,
Surrender all to be made whole.

[Outro]

When fearful thoughts are intertwined,
You calm the storm inside my mind.
Jesus, though I cannot see Your hand,
Your faithful heart I understand.
