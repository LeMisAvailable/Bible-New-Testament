The Cross

Matthew Chapter 27

Duration: ~2 minutes | Style: Powerful Lament

[Verse 1]

Before Pilate, Jesus stood accused,
The crowd chose Barabbas, the Savior refused,
"Crucify Him!" the voices cried,
Pilate washed his hands, stepped aside.
They mocked Him with thorns and a purple robe,
The King of the Jews bore the world's heavy load.

[Chorus]

At Golgotha, the sky turned dark,
Three hours of night left their mark,
"My God, My God, why have You forsaken Me?"
The greatest love the world would ever see.

[Verse 2]

Simon carried the cross up the hill,
They nailed Him there against His will,
Between two thieves, the Savior hung,
While mockery flowed from every tongue.
"Save Yourself if You're truly the One!"
But He stayed—for salvation to be won.

[Chorus]

At Golgotha, the sky turned dark,
Three hours of night left their mark,
"My God, My God, why have You forsaken Me?"
The greatest love the world would ever see.

[Bridge]

The temple curtain tore in two,
The earth shook and the centurion knew,
"Surely this was the Son of God,"
In a borrowed tomb, they laid His body, flawed.

[Outro]

When fearful thoughts are intertwined,
You calm the storm inside my mind.
Jesus, though I cannot see Your hand,
Your faithful heart I understand.
