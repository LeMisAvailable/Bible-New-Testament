Woe to the Hypocrites

Matthew Chapter 23

Duration: ~2 minutes | Style: Intense Dramatic

[Verse 1]

The teachers of the law sit in Moses' seat,
Do what they say, but their actions you shouldn't repeat,
Heavy loads they bind on others' backs,
But won't lift a finger to lighten the packs.
They love the places of honor, the greetings in the square,
But the greatest among you serves everywhere.

[Chorus]

Woe to you who shut the kingdom's door,
You won't go in, and you block those who implore,
Clean the inside, not just the outside show,
True righteousness is what God wants to know.

[Verse 2]

Whitewashed tombs, beautiful outside,
But full of bones and death inside,
You strain out gnats but swallow a camel whole,
Neglecting justice, mercy for the soul.
You build the tombs of prophets you would kill,
History repeats when hearts are still.

[Chorus]

Woe to you who shut the kingdom's door,
You won't go in, and you block those who implore,
Clean the inside, not just the outside show,
True righteousness is what God wants to know.

[Bridge]

Jerusalem, Jerusalem, who kills the prophets sent,
How often I longed to gather you, but you wouldn't relent,
Like a hen gathers her chicks beneath her wings,
But your house is left desolate, empty of kings.

[Outro]

When fearful thoughts are intertwined,
You calm the storm inside my mind.
Jesus, though I cannot see Your hand,
Your faithful heart I understand.
