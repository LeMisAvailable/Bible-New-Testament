The Shipwreck

Acts Chapter 27

Duration: ~2 minutes | Style: Devotional Ballad

[Verse 1]

They set sail for Italy across the sea,
Paul warned them danger waited endlessly,
But the centurion heeded other voices more,
They left Fair Havens seeking distant shore.
Then Euroclydon, the tempest fierce arose,
Fourteen days of terror no one chose.

[Chorus]

Take heart, take courage through the storm tonight,
An angel stood beside me in the fading light,
Not one life shall be lost upon this sea,
God has granted all who sail with me.

[Verse 2]

They threw the cargo overboard to save,
The ship that barely rode each crashing wave,
No sun or stars for many days appeared,
All hope of being rescued disappeared.
But Paul stood up and spoke what God had shown,
"I believe we'll reach dry land, every one."

[Chorus]

Take heart, take courage through the storm tonight,
An angel stood beside me in the fading light,
Not one life shall be lost upon this sea,
God has granted all who sail with me.

[Bridge]

The ship ran aground and broke apart,
Yet every soul reached shore with grateful heart,
Two hundred seventy-six souls made land,
Held safe within the Almighty's hand.

[Outro]

When fearful thoughts are intertwined,
You calm the storm inside my mind.
Jesus, though I cannot see Your hand,
Your faithful heart I understand.
