Appeal to Caesar

Acts Chapter 25

Duration: ~2 minutes | Style: Devotional Ballad

[Verse 1]

Festus took the seat that Felix left,
The Jews pressed charges, filled with bitter cleft,
"Send him to Jerusalem," they cried,
Planning ambush on the countryside.
But Paul knew danger lurked along that way,
And claimed his right that fateful day.

[Chorus]

I appeal to Caesar, let my voice be heard,
Before the emperor I'll speak the Word,
No earthly court can silence what I say,
The risen Christ will have His way.

[Verse 2]

King Agrippa came with Bernice bright,
To hear the prisoner speak that night,
Festus said, "I have no charge to write,
This man has done nothing worthy of death's might."
Great pomp and splendor filled the hall,
Yet Paul stood taller than them all.

[Chorus]

I appeal to Caesar, let my voice be heard,
Before the emperor I'll speak the Word,
No earthly court can silence what I say,
The risen Christ will have His way.

[Bridge]

What seemed like failure, chains and loss,
Became the path to bear the cross,
To Rome he'd go, just as God planned,
The gospel spreading through every land.

[Outro]

When fearful thoughts are intertwined,
You calm the storm inside my mind.
Jesus, though I cannot see Your hand,
Your faithful heart I understand.
