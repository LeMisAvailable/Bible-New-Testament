Taming the Tongue

James Chapter 3

Duration: ~2 minutes | Style: Devotional Ballad

[Verse 1]

The tongue is small but boasts of great things,
Like a spark that sets a forest blazing,
A bit controls the horse's might,
A rudder steers the ship through night.
Yet no one tames this restless flame,
That blesses God and curses in the same.

[Chorus]

Wisdom from above is pure and true,
Peaceable and gentle, merciful too,
No bitter envy, no selfish pride,
Let heaven's wisdom be your guide.

[Verse 2]

Can a spring pour bitter and sweet as one?
Can a fig tree bear olives in the sun?
From the same mouth blessing and curse flow,
But this should never, ever be so.
The fruit of righteousness is peace,
Sown by those who make all conflict cease.

[Chorus]

Wisdom from above is pure and true,
Peaceable and gentle, merciful too,
No bitter envy, no selfish pride,
Let heaven's wisdom be your guide.

[Bridge]

Earthly wisdom breeds disorder's way,
But wisdom from above lights up the day,
Full of mercy, bearing fruit so bright,
Impartial, sincere, walking in the light.

[Outro]

When fearful thoughts are intertwined,
You calm the storm inside my mind.
Jesus, though I cannot see Your hand,
Your faithful heart I understand.
