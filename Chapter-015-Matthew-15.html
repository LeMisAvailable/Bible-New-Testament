Heart Over Tradition

Matthew Chapter 15

Duration: ~2 minutes | Style: Contemporary Worship

[Verse 1]

The Pharisees questioned with hands unwashed,
But Jesus knew their hearts were lost,
"You honor Me with lips alone,
But your hearts are far from My throne.
It's not what enters the mouth that stains,
But what comes out—the heart's remains."

[Chorus]

Create in me a pure heart, O Lord,
Cleanse me from within by Your Word,
Not traditions of men but truth divine,
Transform my heart to be like Thine.

[Verse 2]

A Canaanite woman cried out in pain,
"Lord, heal my daughter, don't let her remain!"
At first silence, then a test of faith,
"Even dogs eat crumbs," she embraced,
"Woman, great is your faith!" He said,
And her daughter rose, healed from her bed.

[Chorus]

Create in me a pure heart, O Lord,
Cleanse me from within by Your Word,
Not traditions of men but truth divine,
Transform my heart to be like Thine.

[Bridge]

Four thousand fed on a mountainside,
Seven loaves and fish multiplied,
Seven baskets gathered, nothing lost,
Our God provides at any cost.

[Outro]

When fearful thoughts are intertwined,
You calm the storm inside my mind.
Jesus, though I cannot see Your hand,
Your faithful heart I understand.
