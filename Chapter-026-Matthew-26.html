The Last Supper

Matthew Chapter 26

Duration: ~2 minutes | Style: Solemn Worship

[Verse 1]

A woman poured perfume on His head,
Preparing Him for what lay ahead,
Judas plotted silver for a kiss,
Thirty pieces for a night like this.
At the table, bread was broken there,
"This is My body," given with care.

[Chorus]

This cup is the blood of the covenant new,
Poured out for many, for me and for you,
Do this in remembrance of what I have done,
The Father's will, not mine, be done.

[Verse 2]

In Gethsemane, He prayed alone,
Sorrow overwhelmed Him to the bone,
"If possible, let this cup pass by,
Yet not My will—Yours till I die."
Three times He prayed while disciples slept,
The weight of the world, the promise He kept.

[Chorus]

This cup is the blood of the covenant new,
Poured out for many, for me and for you,
Do this in remembrance of what I have done,
The Father's will, not mine, be done.

[Bridge]

A kiss of betrayal in the garden night,
Arrested by torches, swords in the light,
Peter denied Him three times before dawn,
The rooster crowed—hope seemed gone.

[Outro]

When fearful thoughts are intertwined,
You calm the storm inside my mind.
Jesus, though I cannot see Your hand,
Your faithful heart I understand.
