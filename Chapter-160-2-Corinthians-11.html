Paul's Sufferings

2 Corinthians Chapter 11

Duration: ~2 minutes | Style: Devotional Ballad

[Verse 1]

I am jealous for you with a godly jealousy,
Promised to one husband, pure in destiny,
But I fear your minds may be led astray,
From your sincere devotion, turning away.
False apostles masquerade as servants of light,
But their end will match their deeds in the night.

[Chorus]

Beaten, imprisoned, shipwrecked at sea,
Hungry and cold, yet Paul stood free,
In weakness and hardship, his faith never failed,
Through suffering and danger, Christ's love prevailed,
In all of his trials, the gospel he hailed.

[Verse 2]

Five times forty lashes minus one I received,
Three times beaten with rods, yet still I believed,
Stoned and shipwrecked, adrift on the sea,
In danger from rivers and bandits, still free.
Sleepless nights and hunger I've known,
Yet the love for the churches has only grown.

[Chorus]

Beaten, imprisoned, shipwrecked at sea,
Hungry and cold, yet Paul stood free,
In weakness and hardship, his faith never failed,
Through suffering and danger, Christ's love prevailed,
In all of his trials, the gospel he hailed.

[Bridge]

If I must boast, I'll boast of my weakness,
The God who sees knows my meekness,
Lowered in a basket through the wall,
Escaping death at my Lord's call.

[Outro]

When fearful thoughts are intertwined,
You calm the storm inside my mind.
Jesus, though I cannot see Your hand,
Your faithful heart I understand.
